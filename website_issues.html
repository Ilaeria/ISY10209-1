Skills: Web Development - Website Issues

When creating a website there are many different issues to consider, such as security, privacy, ethics, copyright, and cultural sensitivity. Listed below are the issues considered when creating this website.

Security and Access Control
This website is publically available, as it is my public portfolio. Access to edit the website requires authentication.
Virus/Malware Attacks
This website is hosted on a secure server by the university. It should therefore have up-to-date virus and malware protection.
Copyright and Intellectual Property
To protect myself from copyright issues, I have been careful to only include content I have created myself. If content from elsewhere was required, it has been appropriately attributed.
Privacy
The only private information displayed on this website is my own. I have only included photos of myself and my pets. I ensured that my resume page does not include the private information of other people. The "Kings Music Store" example form does not store private information.
Defamation
This is my own personal portfolio and does not contain ny information that may be damaging to another entity.
Societal and Cultural Considerations
It's important to remember that websites are accessible worldwide. While this website is primarily targeted at a specific Australian audience, it may be accessed by anyone, anywhere in the world. Further, Australia is a multicultural society. You cannot assume that something which is acceptable for you will always be acceptable for others, no matter how small your audience.

It was also important to me to be respectful of the Aboriginal and Torres Straight Islander communities of Australia. I read publications from both Queensland (Queensland Government Department of Communities & Office for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Partnerships 2005) and New South Wales (Aboriginal Services Branch & Aboriginal Reference Group 2009) governments. I learned, for example, that you should be respectful of deceased people and not show images of them or refer to them by name.

List of References

New South Wales Department of Community Services 2009, Working with Aboriginal people and communities: A practice resource, New South Wales Department of Community Services, Ashfield, NSW.

Queensland Government Department of Communities 2005, Engaging Queenslanders: Introduction to working with Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities, Queensland Government Department of Communities, QLD.
