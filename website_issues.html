Skills: Web Development - Website Issues

Website Issues

When creating a website there are many different issues to consider, such as security, privacy, ethics, copyright, and cultural sensitivity. Listed below are the issues considered when creating this website.

Security and Access Control
This website is publically available, as it is my public portfolio. Access to edit the website requires authentication.
Virus/Malware Attacks
This website is hosted on a secure server by the university. It should therefore have up-to-date virus and malware protection.
Copyright and Intellectual Property
To protect myself from copyright issues, I have been careful to only include content I have created myself. If content from elsewhere was required, it has been appropriately attributed.
Privacy
The only private information displayed on this website is my own. I have only included photos of myself and my pets. I ensured that my resume page does not include the private information of other people. The "Kings Music Store" example form does not store private information.
Defamation
This is my own personal portfolio and does not contain ny information that may be damaging to another entity.
Societal and Cultural Considerations
It's important to remember that websites are accessible worldwide. While this website is primarily targeted at a specific Australian audience, it may be accessed by anyone, anywhere in the world. Further, Australia is a multicultural society. You cannot assume that something which is acceptable for you will always be acceptable for others, no matter how small your audience.

It was also important to me to be respectful of the Aboriginal and Torres Straight Islander communities of Australia. I read publications from both Queensland (Queensland Government Department of Communities & Office for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Partnerships 2005) and New South Wales (Aboriginal Services Branch & Aboriginal Reference Group 2009) governments. I learned, for example, that you should be respectful of deceased people and not show images of them or refer to them by name. Therefore, I was careful not to mention any deceased relatives in my biography.

GoDaddy
GoDaddy is a very well-known domain name registrar and was the only brand name I immediately recognised of the 3 options considered. However, they are registered in the USA, not Australia.

GoDaddy offers different plans for Windows or Linux operating systems, but the prices and features appear to be comparable. They didn't specify the web server for either type of plan; it's assumed they are running Apache on Linux plans and IIS on Windows plans.

Their price advertised was "$4.99/month on sale, $10.99/month when you renew". The ongoing price is listed in the above table. You can get the cheaper price if you sign up for 12/24/36 months in advance. Their minimum renewal period is 3 months.

It's not clear if GoDaddy offers daily backups for free or if you have to pay: they offer a paid "Site Backup & Restore" feature for $2.99/month, but that may just be for an assisted service.

GoDaddy ranked clear first in 5 categories and first equal in 3 categories.
Digital Pacific
Digital Pacific is an Australian company, providing carbon neutral web hosting options. They provide a lot of different plans.

Digital Pacific ranked clear first in 3 categories and first equal 3 categories.
UberGlobal
UberGlobal's website and plan options were clear and simple. They are a large Australian cloud hosting provider.

UberGlobal ranked clear first in 1 category considered and first equal in 4 categories.

Recommendation

GoDaddy was the only provider reviewed to offer either Linux or Windows plans, and at a comparable price. Even though they are not an Australian company, they are well-known and reliable and offer a range of services to suit clients with different needs. Based on this reseach, I would choose GoDaddy as my hosting provider.
